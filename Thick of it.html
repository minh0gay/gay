A little bit about KSI

Olajide Olayinka Williams "JJ" Olatunji (born 19 June, 1993), better known by his online alias KSI, is an English social media influencer, professional boxer, and musician. He is a co-founding member of YouTube group the Sidemen, the CEO of Misfits Boxing, and the co-owner of Prime Hydration, XIX Vodka, Sidemen Clothing, restaurant chain Sides, cereal brand Best Breakfasts, and pre-made lunch brand Lunchly.

He registered his main YouTube channel in 2009 and built a following through his gaming commentary videos of the FIFA video game series. His content later diversified to include vlogs and comedy-style videos. As of October 2022, he had over 41 million subscribers and over 10 billion video views across his three YouTube channels. In 2021, he was listed among Forbes 30 Under 30 for the Europe Entertainment category. He has also ventured into acting, featuring in the British comedy film Laid in America in 2016. He was the subject of KSI: Can't Lose in 2018, a documentary film following the build-up to his first boxing match, and KSI: In Real Life in 2023, an Amazon Prime Video documentary film following his life from June 2021 to October 2022.

Creating rap music since 2011, he released his debut studio album, Dissimulation, in 2020, which debuted at number 2 on the UK Albums Chart. His second studio album, All Over the Place, was released in 2021 and debuted at number 1. He has achieved fourteen top 40 singles on the UK Singles Chart, seven of them ending up in the Top 10 and five of them ending up in the Top 5.

A Disjointed Opening

KSI's "Thick Of It" featuring Trippie Redd is a bit of a mixed bag. While the track has some redeeming qualities, it ultimately falls short of expectations. The song opens with a clean guitar riff that quickly gives way to KSI's vocals, which, unfortunately, sound sloppy and over-processed. The layered vocals feel disjointed and lack cohesion, creating a messy soundscape.

Fleeting Feature and Lackluster Energy

As the song progresses, it transitions into a pop-infused track with glimpses of Trippie Redd's vocals. However, KSI's energy feels lacking, and the verses are too short, giving the impression that the chorus arrives prematurely. Trippie Redd's feature injects much-needed energy and passion into the song, but his contribution is fleeting, leaving the listener wanting more. A longer feature from Trippie Redd could have significantly elevated the track.

Lyrical Limitations

Lyrically, "Thick Of It" touches upon KSI's journey from YouTube to music and boxing, highlighting his success and resilience. However, the lyrics lack depth and originality, relying on familiar tropes about overcoming adversity and achieving greatness. The chorus, while catchy, is repetitive and becomes tiresome after a few listens.

An Unfulfilled Promise

"Thick Of It" is a decent attempt by KSI to create a catchy pop song with a hip-hop edge. However, the track suffers from several shortcomings, including KSI's underwhelming vocal performance, the brevity of Trippie Redd's feature, and the lackluster lyrics. While the song may appeal to die-hard KSI fans, it's unlikely to make a lasting impact on the wider music scene.

KSI Thick Of It Review

"Thick Of It" is a passable but ultimately forgettable track that fails to showcase KSI's full potential as a musician. While the song has its moments, it's not a particularly strong addition to KSI's discography.
